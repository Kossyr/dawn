7th November 1987 - It's not looking good. We are pushing again towards Montignac but it costs us a lot and the enemy is still superior I find. Command believes this offensive will finally break them, but I am not so sure if we don't break first...

An offensive to end the war and push the enemy off the island starts this morning.

The main offensive target is the city of Montignac. You are a part of the eastern front which serves as a decoy. You shall attack first and make the enemy believe our main push will be in the east, meanwhile we will strike from the west and in the centre.

You start here, very near the enemy positions.

Attack and seize a small farm together with squads Boris and Leonid.

Wait for the tanks from squad Fyodor to arrive, they will take you and squad Boris towards your second objective - the village of Entre Deux. Leonid will meanwhile seize the coastal base.

The enemy has fortified a hill which you will have to break through before you can attack the village itself.

Seize the enemy village of Entre Deux.

Retreat back to Provins.


Mission completed?


The decoy plan was a failure as the enemy has outflanked us and obliterated our western front. Our push towards Entre Deux did not mean anything then. A general retreat has been declared as our position is no longer sustainable. Get as fast as you can to Saint Pierre, comrade!
