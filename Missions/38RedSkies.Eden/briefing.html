2nd February 1989 - Dearest Ekaterina,

this is the eighteenth letter I am writing to you since your last answer. My comrades keep telling me that I am a fool and that I worry for nothing. I trust my feelings though and I feel that something is amiss. I'll keep nagging the postmaster until ke spills the truth. If there is an issue, and I believe there is, he surely knows about it.

Since my last letter, I have flown in the latest attack helicopter model and participated in a somewhat dangerous mission. You don't have to worry, I was not injured unlike many others. I saved a trapped squad and afterwards befriended their commander, Sergey Rashiv. I hope one day I am able to introduce you to him.

One last attack separates us from victory. Today, the war should finally end. I might have to remain here for a few weeks before I'm given a leave, but like I promised, I would return the moment I am able to. And that moment might not be so far off now.

I hope I will have good news for you in my next letter.

Forever yours,
Nikolay

The battle of Everon concludes today. The enemy was pushed south and now only holds Saint Pierre and few surrounding villages. Our ground forces are getting ready to mount the final assault against enemy positions to end this war once and for all. You shall provide them with air support.

You start with your squadron at the field heliport near Montignac.

Get in your V-80 attack helicopter.

Follow your squadron leader to the frontlines where our forces are preparing to mount the final attack.

Provide air support for our ground forces. Help them seize enemy positions in Regina, Durras, Vernon and finally the port of Saint Pierre.

Return back to the field heliport and land.


Mission completed


Everon is ours. After long and hard fought battles, we pushed the enemy back to his home island of Malden. We brought peace to the archipelago once more, in no small part thanks to you, comrade Ablomov!
