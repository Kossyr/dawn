13th December 1987 - Dearest Ekaterina,

I hope this letter finds you well. I was hoping to get back to the mainland, but alas, I must break my promise that I have given to return till the end of the year. The army will not let me. There is a war right now - or so we believe. It is not yet official, although surely you must have heard something back in Russia. The NATO and we once again exchange blows like seven years ago.

They pushed us to the brink of collapse, but right now it seems we are slowly turning the tide. You do not have to worry - I am still flying the transport helicopters only, although I wish to be assigned to attack squadrons. It is after all my dream and I am close to accomplishing it. I do hope you forgive me my indecency of risking my life while I promised my heart and soul to you. I am sworn to you, my dearest Ekaterina, now and always, and I will come back to you. But not presently.

Leaves are on hold until we deal with the enemy - though I will ask for one as soon as I am able to even if the attack squadron calls me for duty the day before. I will come the moment I can, dearest Ekaterina.

I long for your emerald eyes and dazzling smile. I think of you everyday and I ache for your tender touch. This war will not keep us apart for I will not let it. I shall return soon, I promise you that.

Forever yours,
Nikolay

Today's flight plan is quite routine, comrade Ablomov - you and your co-pilot are tasked with transporting newly arrived soldiers to the front lines.

You start in the northern heliport in Severovodsk.

Pick up recently arrived squad in Chernoborg.

Unload the group at the front lines in Malsk.

Destroy incoming tanks.

Pick up survivors from Astrabirsk.

Return to heliport in Severovodsk.


Mission completed


Good flying and aim, comrade Ablomov. Your skills are wasted in the transport helicopters.
